# OSS Program Readiness

## What the Project Does

`ai-offer-starter` is an open source toolkit for turning AI-assisted deliverables into ethical, specific, sellable, and deliverable service offers.

It includes documentation, templates, examples, JSON schemas, and small TypeScript helpers for offer readiness, pricing, validation, outreach, delivery, and simple unit economics.

## Why It Is Open Source

The project is open source because the problem is educational and community-shaped. People need reusable patterns, safer examples, and practical language for selling AI-assisted work without hype.

Open source makes the standards visible:

- no guaranteed income claims
- no spam automation
- no private data scraping
- no hidden paid API dependency
- no magic positioning

## Who It Helps

- AI builders who can create useful deliverables but need offer structure.
- Freelancers who want clearer service packaging.
- Educators teaching practical AI work.
- Communities trying to reduce scammy AI business advice.
- Small service providers who need ethical templates and checklists.

## Problem It Solves

Many people can generate content, designs, automations, dashboards, landing pages, or reports with AI. They often struggle to choose a real customer, define a real problem, price the work, validate demand, sell without spam, and deliver with quality.

This toolkit gives them a small, practical path from deliverable to offer.

## What It Does Not Promise

The project does not promise:

- easy money
- guaranteed income
- passive income
- search ranking improvements
- more bookings or sales
- automated business success
- permission to spam prospects

This is not a money printer. This is an offer design and delivery toolkit.

## Ethical Risks

- Users may copy templates and add exaggerated claims.
- Pricing helpers may be mistaken for financial advice.
- Outreach examples may be misused for unsolicited bulk messaging.
- New examples could drift into hype unless reviewed carefully.

## Maintainer Notes

Keep the project small and legible. Prefer better docs, clearer examples, stronger validation, and safer contribution standards over new features.

Reject contributions that depend on spam, private data misuse, dark patterns, or guaranteed income claims.
